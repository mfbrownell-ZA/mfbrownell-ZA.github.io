1 group nice to have

First — thank you. The progress from v1 to v2 has been genuinely impressive. The language across the app is clearer, the navigation is cleaner, and the structural changes to the contact form and dashboard are exactly what we asked for. We're excited about where this is heading and feel like we're building something really worthwhile together. Below is what we need to get across the line before pilot, plus some context on decisions we've made and questions we'd like your input on.

Context

Decisions, Open Items & Questions

Decisions Made
  • Maximum of 20 contacts per phone
  • Up to 3 emergency contacts, drawn from within the 20 — not additional slots
  • No automatic connection to 10111 or any emergency services — only parent-approved emergency contacts
  • No call transcripts and no access to call recordings for parents
  • Parents must accept Terms & Conditions as part of account creation
  • Once-off call bundles should not expire (to confirm with Snaptel)
Not Yet Tested
  • Actual functionality of quiet hours and contact whitelisting on a working phone
  • Device auto-provisioning and WiFi setup flows (coming in the next build)
  • Payments with a live payment processor
  • Minutes and bundles functionality end-to-end
  • Invoicing
  • Admin portal (our side)
These are all on the roadmap — flagging so both sides are aligned on what's been validated and what hasn't.
Open Questions for Snaptel
  • Minutes and bundles visibility in app: How will remaining call value be surfaced to parents? Will each call log entry show a running total and remaining balance, or does this live somewhere else in the app? We need this to be clear and parent-friendly — not a raw data dump.
  • TickeyBox-to-TickeyBox calls: If a parent adds a contact that is another TickeyBox number, can we detect this automatically and flag those calls as free? This is a meaningful feature for families with more than one device or for children calling friends who also have TickeyBoxes.

4 Emergency contacts — toggle on contact card, max 3, bypasses quiet hours
Add a Contact form / Contact card

There is currently no way to designate a contact as an emergency contact. The use case: certain contacts (e.g. grandparent, other parent) should be able to call through even during quiet hours and weekly schedule blocks.

Fix Required
Add an "Emergency contact" toggle to the contact card (add and edit views). Rules:
Toggle behaviour
Defaults OFF. When turned ON, the contact bypasses quiet hours and weekly schedule restrictions — they can always call through.
Maximum 3 emergency contacts per phone
Once 3 are set, the toggle on all other contacts is disabled with a note: "You've set 3 emergency contacts — the maximum. Remove one to add another."
Visual treatment
Emergency contacts should be visually distinct in the contacts list — e.g. a small badge or coloured indicator — so the parent can see at a glance which contacts have this status.
Current contact form
First name Last name Phone number Can call this phone [toggle] Matt's Phone can call them [toggle]
Required — add emergency toggle
First name Last name Phone number Can call this phone [toggle] Matt's Phone can call them [toggle] Emergency contact [toggle] Can always call through, even during quiet hours.

11 RICA — preferred location is onboarding, not Account Settings
Account Settings (current) → Onboarding (preferred)

RICA is a legal requirement but currently lives in Account Settings — most parents won't find it there. This is not a launch blocker, but our preference is for it to be surfaced in the onboarding flow so parents encounter it early and naturally. The non-negotiable in either case: it must never block account access. Parents must always be able to skip and come back.

Preferred (not a launch blocker)
Ideally, add RICA as a step in the onboarding flow immediately after account creation, with a "I'll do this later" option. If deprioritised for pilot, ensure a persistent dashboard banner is shown until complete: "Action needed: Upload your ID and proof of address to activate your phone service."
