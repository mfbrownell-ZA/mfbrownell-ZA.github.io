Update TickeyBox — Where Things Stand ~15 min
Matt gives a brief update on the TickeyBox side — demand, pilot pipeline, and timeline. This sets the context for why the items being discussed today matter and why the April timeline is the target.
  • 📋
    Waitlist and demand — 325 households, all organic The waitlist is at 325 and still growing entirely through word of mouth — no paid acquisition has been run. A notable pattern has emerged: people are signing up in friendship groups of 5–10, effectively self-organising into calling pods. The motivation is getting TickeyBox-to-TickeyBox free calling across a friend group, which is validating the network effect assumption at the core of the product design.
  • 🏠
    Pilot cohort — ~40 households confirmed and ready We have approximately 40 households ready for the paid pilot — slightly below the original 50-household target but a strong cohort. Predominantly Western Cape with a small number of households in Johannesburg. The pilot will be paid, at a low introductory price. This cohort is ready to go as soon as the app build is in a position to support it.
  • 🐶
    Dogfooding — ready but currently blocked We have 8 Grandstream GHP611W units in hand and the dogfood participant group is lined up. We are ready to start as soon as the app build allows it. No dogfooding has happened yet — the app build is the sole blocker at this point. This makes the build completion date and the provisioning story the critical path for everything that follows.
  • 📦
    Hardware and supply chain — in good shape TickeyBox is now an official reseller through First Distribution. Grandstream are actively supporting the pilot order for the 50-unit cohort, with a backup supply option in place if needed. Lead time for the pilot units is approximately 2–3 weeks from order. For the full launch, conversations are underway with Grandstream on a 1,000-unit order — and that order needs to be placed 8–10 weeks before the intended launch date, which sets the hardware decision deadline.
  • 🗓
    Timeline — everything gates on the app build Every other part of the TickeyBox side is in motion: the pilot cohort is confirmed, hardware supply is arranged, and dogfood participants are standing by. The single constraint is the app build. Once that is ready, dogfooding starts immediately, followed by the paid pilot. The 1,000-unit launch order has an 8–10 week lead time, which means the go/no-go decision for full launch needs to be made while the pilot is still running — not after it finishes.

Post-pilot Parking Lot — Post-Pilot Conversations

🅿 Not for today

The items below are genuinely important but premature to resolve before we have a live product and real usage data. The right time to have these conversations is after the pilot — once we know the product works, users love it, and the commercial opportunity is clearer for everyone. We're noting them here so they don't get lost, but they're not on today's agenda.
Longer-term commercial partnership structure
Hannes mentioned the idea of a deeper commercial arrangement — possibly including equity or a more formal revenue-sharing model — as the relationship matures. This is a conversation worth having once the pilot has proven demand and the product is operating at scale. No need to define terms before we've launched.
Volume-based commercial renegotiation
As household numbers grow, the commercial terms will naturally evolve. A checkpoint at 500 households or 12 months of operation makes sense — revisit pricing, SLA scope, and the shape of the partnership at that point.
Product roadmap alignment — Year 2
There are likely features and capabilities (messaging, group calls, parental notifications, desktop app) that become relevant post-pilot. The right time to align on what SnapTel builds vs what TickeyBox builds is once we have clear signal from pilot users on what matters most.
