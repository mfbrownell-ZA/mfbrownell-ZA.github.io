Founder's Read

The sprint exposed three structural truths. First, Jem closes well but activates slowly — the gap between signed contract and live, earning revenue is the biggest drag on MRR targets across every initiative. Second, on-site human contact is systematically underweighted across sales and marketing, despite data that makes the case plainly: 3× faster conversion, one-third the cancellation rate. Third, the team has the execution capability — when given clear scope and the right tools (Claude Code, customer validation, design partnerships), things move fast. The failure modes are almost always upstream: wrong metric, undocumented scope, missing DRI, or assumed trust in a segment that needs to earn it.

Highest-Conviction Insights

The 3 signals that matter

SIGNAL 01
3×
On-site vs digital conversion rate
On-site activations close in 3 days. Chatbot takes 38. On-site cancels at 3.7%. Chatbot at 13.1%. This is the most important number in the retros and it's being systematically underweighted in resource allocation. Every activation plan that doesn't start with on-site is working against the data.
SIGNAL 02
3–4mo
Avg gap between signed deal and revenue
Enterprise onboarding consistently runs 3–4 months from contract to earning MRR. Rohloff: signed, R0 revenue. Bidvest: R480K signed, R10K live. Servest: R420K signed, live 6 April. Sprint MRR targets need to account for this pipeline lag structurally — not be surprised by it.
SIGNAL 03
62.9%
Mobile contracts from 3 employers
Meridian (604), Stallion (273), Marshall (154) = 62.9% of active mobile contracts. Stallion already showed what one relationship breakdown costs: 42 contracts flagged as unable to deduct, R100K+ exposure. Client concentration is the silent structural risk no retro addressed directly.

Blind Spots

Structural risks no retro owned

⚡
Client concentration in Mobile
Meridian (604), Stallion (273), and Marshall (154) account for 62.9% of all active mobile contracts. The Stallion reconciliation crisis (42 contracts unable to deduct, R100K+ exposure) showed what one relationship breakdown costs. No retro named this as a strategic risk to diversify. It needs to be an explicit next-sprint goal, not an implicit hope.
📉
Fundraise valuation ahead of market
£1.2M of £1.5M committed with the explicit acknowledgement that the valuation is ahead of where the market is. The retro commits to "shifting narrative to shareholder value creation" — but the target metrics (R6M MRR by June, R7.2M by September) are now the fundraise's credibility collateral. Any shortfall compounds. This is the constraint that shapes everything else's priority.
🔁
Accountability is still person-dependent, not system-dependent
Multiple retros (AI-Native Team, Dream 200, T&A AI, Enterprise) name the same gap: accountability runs through leadership pushing, not through systems that enforce it. Simon-driven exec relationships aren't a system. Pipeline reviews that flag issues but don't enforce consequences aren't a system. The sprint delivered culture; it hasn't yet delivered infrastructure.
Aggregated Commitments

What the team is building next

These are the recurring commitments from the "Going Forward" sections, grouped by theme. The overlap is notable — 5 separate retros independently converged on written scope and clear DRI as a fix. The sprint may have a discipline problem at the system level, not the individual level.

Activation & Sales
Scale on-site activation as primary sales channel
Build a standardised activation marketing kit (pre-visit comms, day-of materials, post-activation sequence). Every priority client gets an on-site activation cadence. Monthly visit cadence for top accounts.
Sales Process
Enforce pipeline playbook discipline
DME and Q&N stages as primary pipeline coverage metric. Deals above R100K require an executive WhatsApp intro. Mutual action plans required for every Q&N-stage deal. Stale deals escalated before they become January.
Onboarding
Pre-launch readiness checklist with hard deadlines
Client-side comms obligations and sign-off before go-live is confirmed. RM and EWA limits documented and signed as part of contract. Tiered onboarding paths: Fast Track (<30 days) vs Slow Track (30–90 days with PM).
Product
Written brief before any build begins
Objective, target state, definition of done, explicit out-of-scope. Manual operational processes documented alongside code. Applies to every new initiative — no exceptions after the Noloco lesson.
AI & Product
AI-first design for all new T&A features
Every new feature starts with: "What would this look like if AI was the primary interface?" The Late No-Show Agent ships to production. Group-photo-based clock-in validated with 2+ design partners.
Engineering
Structured integration PM framework
Every integration gets shared timeline, named owners, explicit dependencies, traffic-light status visible to commercial. Client-side delay past 5 days → automatic escalation. SLA commitments in every integration scope.
Marketing
Two distinct customer journeys
Existing Jem customers (trust baseline) vs new-to-Jem (no trust). Separate campaign templates, messaging, and channel strategy for each. Surface Jem Mobile at payslip delivery and VAS data purchase moments.
People & AI
Measurable AI outcomes per machine
Clear expectations of AI use per function. Clear outcomes: agents deployed, time saved, ZAR saved. Clear measurement cadence (daily, weekly). Accountability moves from Simon pushing to system enforcing.
JemX
Spin JemX out with single commercial mandate
R500K profit by end of Q3 (R2.5M revenue over 6 months). One goal, one P&L, one accountability line. Innovation value becomes a byproduct of commercial work — not a parallel objective competing for definition.
Dream 200
CRM hygiene sprint + standing updates
Zero companies without a stage by end of March. Correct all "Uncontacted" companies with clear evidence of engagement. Dream 200 stage updates become a standing item in every weekly pod check-in.
Finance
Implement cost-saving actions + roll out efficiency metrics
Priority on WhatsApp and Jem Mobile cost reduction. ARR per employee and employee efficiency metrics rolled out with machine leads. Stitch as primary, Paysoft as backup only.
Revenue
Hit the investor milestones
Investors have visibility: R6M MRR by end of June, R7.2M by end of September. These aren't just internal targets — they're the valuation's credibility collateral. Everything else is secondary to these two numbers.
