The deliverables materially changed this assessment. The Securex deck is the work of someone who thinks in campaigns, not logistics — a 12-step pre-event plan, a 6-step stand engagement flow with qualification built in, lead tiering, retargeting, a "Missed us at Securex" campaign, and a closing frame that explicitly positions the event as a full-funnel pipeline engine. Night Watch, Underground, Production Line, with a "Jem Moment" embedded in each. A voiceover script that lands the dual-sided value in two sentences. A PR angle Jem has never used. That is narrative thinking. It is the kind of instinct that, in the right role, becomes a brand platform.

The gaps are specific and honest. She does not yet set hard commercial metrics — she talks correctly about pipeline without naming a number, and frames event success in directional terms rather than accountable ones. Her AI fluency is practitioner-level, not systems-level; she uses tools excellently but she is not yet building automations or thinking about how to replace headcount with agents. And her career has been shaped by agencies and large organisations more than scrappy operator environments.

None of these are disqualifying. They are the gaps you would close in a well-structured 90-day onboarding with clear commercial targets and access to the AI tooling the team has already built. The question for Caroline's session is whether the posture is right — does she arrive as an owner or a learner?

Questions to Resolve in Behavioural Interview

  • Commercial metric ownership: Has she ever owned a pipeline number, a CAC target, or a revenue-attributed metric? If so, what was it, how did she track it, and did she hit it?
  • AI systems vs AI tools: Describe the last workflow she built — not used — with AI. What does it do, how does it run, what did it cost to build? Look for specificity, not aspiration.
  • Builder under constraint: Ask for a time she had to build a marketing function with no agency, no template, and no budget. Not a project. A function.
  • Ownership posture: She mentioned wanting to learn from Jem's AI systems. Probe: is she coming to lead from day one, or is there a ramp period required? The answer matters at this stage.
  • Agency experience as a risk: Most of her career has been inside structures that absorbed risk on her behalf. Ask her directly: what is the hardest decision she's made without a safety net?
