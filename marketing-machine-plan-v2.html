01 — Goals & Milestones

What success looks like
by September.

Six outcomes the machine must deliver.

01
New marketing team in place
Head and Creator hired and operational. This is the unlock for everything else — without the team, the rest of this list is theoretical.
02
Dream 200 pipeline compounding
Relationships tracked, touchpoints logged, meaningful engagements progressing. Every sales and marketing activity maps back to these 200 organisations.
03
Distribution growing through real people
Employee-generated content continues to compound — the team showing up on LinkedIn in ways that feel real. Average weekly impressions >300k. Relationships with a handful of authority figures in our sectors are active and producing signal.
04
Social proof has a repeatable recipe
Phase 1 produced >45 atoms of social proof — we'll know what lands in the coming weeks. By September, we've codified it into a simple process for developing new proof atoms without it being a big production each time. Case study → stat → quote → visual.
05
Product updates reach clients without drama
A simple system — built in collaboration with product — for communicating new features, updates, and launches to Dream 200 clients in the right format. No heavy lift per release. Just a lightweight, consistent loop that keeps the right people informed.
06
Jem Mobile handed off
Success here is that Jem Mobile is no longer core within Marketing — it lives in the product vertical team where it belongs. Marketing provides light-touch support when needed, but this thing has its own engine and its own home.
The Why
Marketing's job is to compound trust so sales gets easier. Six months of solid execution on this list puts us in a credible position to hit R15M MRR and profitability by Q2 2027.
02 — Key Initiatives

What we're actually doing
to get there.

Five things we're actually doing — outside of team structure.

1
Dream 200
Use data to know if things are moving, how they're moving, and what's making them move. Every sales and marketing activity should map to this list — if it doesn't, it probably shouldn't be happening. Which assets work? Which events work? What's missing in the toolbox? A clear rhythm should be in place with sales that takes us closer to ABM.
H1 Priority
2
Employee-Generated Content & Authority Relationships
Expand Flamingo to the full team on EGC and map against Dream200 contacts. Build relationships with a small number of genuine authority figures in our core sectors who will show up and say real things. The goal is authentic signal reaching the right people, not polished content reaching everyone.
H1 Priority
3
Social Proof Recipe
Evolve phase 1 to include some big enterprise clients. Use usage data to codify going forward — build a simple, repeatable recipe (case study → stat → quote → visual) that lets us produce new proof atoms quickly without it becoming a project each time. Then embed them everywhere by default: sales, events, onboarding.
H1 Priority
4
Product Comms System
Build a lightweight system — with product, not around them — for communicating new features, launches, and updates to Dream 200 clients. The right format for the right update, without marketing needing to run a campaign every time something ships. Keep the right people informed. Kill the "I didn't know Jem does that" problem for good.
H1–H2
5
Jem Mobile — Hand It Off
Marketing's job here is to support Jem Mobile's transition into the product vertical — not to own it long-term. We provide light-touch support: materials, messaging, whatever they need to stand on their own. Done well, this frees up capacity and puts Jem Mobile where it actually belongs.
