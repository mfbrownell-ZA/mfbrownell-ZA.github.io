What's not being said loudly enough

The blind spots

Things that appear in the data or between the lines — but aren't getting the same airtime as the named problems.

📉
Weekly active shippers flat at 200 — and nobody owns it
3× traffic, 7,400 prospects in Q1 — but weekly active shippers haven't moved since mid-January. Product flagged it as the most honest volume health metric, then didn't track it as a KR. This is the real activation signal and it's been invisible.
🔄
Rate accuracy is a confirmed churn driver with no fix plan
Checkout vs invoice mismatch is directly linked to at least 4 merchant churns or dissatisfaction cases: Melvill & Moon, Escape Gear, It's All Good, Bambalam. Product flagged it as a P0 trust issue. It doesn't appear in any Q2 OKR draft. That's a gap.
🏭
Economy is a structural single point of failure
One person, no cover, 2 KRs not started, no succession plan. Max's April leave has exposed this clearly. Finance stepping in as temporary cover is the wrong answer. This needs a structured solution before it becomes a crisis.
🧭
TUNL Economy: UK is the logical next market — but focus is the constraint
Multiple big customers have asked for Economy to the UK. The logic holds: lower margin but creates volume — UK express is still faster/pricier enough that Economy doesn't cannibalise. The B2B freight forwarding signal is real. But it's operationally constrained (Max + the international vs Economy focus tradeoff). Decision needed: when, not if.
🐳
Whale retention playbook doesn't exist
Ciovita and Chommies churned in Q1 due to operational gaps — speed and system completeness, not price. That's 2 whales in one quarter. There's no retention dashboard, no early warning system, and no defined response playbook. The next whale at risk is invisible until it's too late.
🛍️
Shopify Marketplace is an untapped authority channel
TUNL has ~150 Shopify users but effectively zero app reviews. DHL has 12 on the same platform. A simple R500-per-review incentive campaign could generate 20–40 reviews with minimal budget. This isn't just about SA discovery — it's the authority foundation needed before any international expansion on Shopify. Currently invisible.
Q2 Planning

Suggested focus areas for Q2

Based on what the retros signal collectively — not just what each function plans to do in isolation.

1. Resolve SCA and close the books
Non-negotiable
Finance is explicitly called out as the biggest company-level threat. SCA resolution unlocks reliable margin analysis, AFS sharing for E4E, better UPS credit terms, and management accounts — simultaneously. Nothing else in Finance moves at the required pace until this is done. Cameron and Chanelle need a hard deadline in April, not May.
→Month-end close within 7 working days — consistently, every month in Q2
→SCA resolution plan with hard deadline agreed by end of April
→Reliable margin analysis and debtors days reporting live by mid-Q2
2. Ship live rates and fix activation properly
Biggest product lever
Customisable live rates is the most important product bet in Q2. It directly addresses the activation blocker identified in Product Discovery. It's already in engineering. Ship it early Q2 and measure properly. Weekly active shippers — not time-to-first-shipment — is the correct health metric. And rate accuracy (checkout vs invoice) needs a P0 plan, not another quarter of deferral.
→Live rates shipped and measured for checkout conversion impact by end of April
→Weekly active shippers as primary product health metric, target 250+
→Rate accuracy P0 resolution plan agreed and in execution
3. Make product marketing the new growth engine
Revenue-critical
Channel-led growth has hit its ceiling — confirmed in the session. TINTB proved that even best-in-class creative doesn't unlock new traffic pools. The pivot: product marketing as the primary motion. Robin and Brett have already started. Bundled feature releases, seasonal campaigns, making what TUNL does visible to the 150+ Shopify users and sizable leads base who still don't know the platform's full capability. SDR: exit decision made.
→Product marketing cadence live: Brett + Robin delivering bundled feature releases (Shopify-style seasonal drops)
→Shopify Marketplace reviews campaign: R500 incentive per review, targeting 20+ reviews from existing 150+ users
→25+ qualified Twhale inbounds — SDR exit decision made; replacement or structural fix required
4. Fix team fragility — two hires, not one
Operational risk
A new CustOps hire addresses three problems: Support capacity, Freya's succession, and Economy cover. The second hire — a full-time product designer — is equally urgent. Steph (fractional, 2 days/week) is good but not a long-term solution; Brett confirmed this in the session. Keep Steph as cover while the search runs. Fundraise budget has been framed to include a product person focused on international — the timing aligns.
→New CustOps hire actioned and onboarding started in April · Freya succession: Phiwe cross-training begins immediately
→Full-time product designer search kicked off — keep Steph as fractional cover during process
→Senior dev performance decision made and actioned — clarity before mid-Q2
5. Make the Twhale motion repeatable
Scale the win
ASP up 55%, revenue/merchant up 90%, 9 whales in March — the upmarket strategy is working. The job now is to make it repeatable and defensible: documented onboarding playbook, a whale retention dashboard before the next churn, and fulfillment partners (Inospace) formalised with clear success metrics. FinnBUILDER, Ellie & May, and The Matie Shop need high-touch ramp plans to cross Tuna threshold in Q2.
→8+ new Tunas and Whales acquired in Q2
→Whale retention dashboard + early warning system live before end of May
→Inospace pilot formalised with defined success metrics and review cadence
6. Fundraise from strength, not pressure
Strategic
E4E conversation in progress for a R7–10M extension round. March recovery (at or above budget) is the narrative. But the fundraise can't close without clean AFSs — which loops directly back to SCA resolution. These are one workstream. Maintain 6+ months runway throughout Q2. Below 6 months, options narrow. Below 3 months, decisions become reactive.
→E4E conversation progressed to clear outcome (term sheet or explicit no) by end of May
→Clean AFSs ready for investor sharing (depends on SCA — same deadline)
→6+ months operating runway maintained throughout Q2
